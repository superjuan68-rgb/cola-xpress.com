Visual Consistency Strategy

Aesthetic Target
Apple product film × A24 documentary. Dark, quiet, deliberate. No bright or busy backgrounds. Every frame should look like it could be a still from Succession Season 4 or an Apple "Shot on iPhone" campaign. The tone is: expensive and quiet. Nothing flashy. The vGrow earns attention by being different from what you expected.
Color Rules
One saturated color in the frame: teal (#38b4c8) from the LED interior. Everything else is desaturated — near-black backgrounds, neutral grays, muted greens on the plant. The only warmth is the Samsung LED bar (warm white) and distant city glow through windows. If a frame has two competing color sources, it's wrong. The teal should always win.
Lighting Model
The vGrow IS the light source in most shots. When the door is closed, the teal seam glow is the only color. When the door is open, the interior LED floods the frame. External light: city window (warm amber), practical lamp (warm, dim), never overhead or flash.
The vGrow Description
Always use: "VIVOSUN vGrow smart grow box, matte black vertical cabinet 18 inches wide 48 inches tall, single door with large rectangular viewing window, premium appliance aesthetic, teal LED seam glow at door edges" Consistency tip: generate your hero vGrow product portrait first (Image 01). Use it as Midjourney style reference (--sref) for every subsequent shot that includes the unit.
The Apartment
Always use: "minimalist modern apartment, charcoal gray walls, dark herringbone wood floor, single large window with blurred night city view, considered furniture — no clutter" Same room, every time. Generate a reference shot of the empty room first. Use as --sref for all interior lifestyle shots.
Camera / Lens
"shot on Sony A7S III, 85mm f/1.4 lens, shallow depth of field, cinematic documentary photography" for portrait and product shots. Switch to "35mm lens" for wide room context shots. "100mm macro lens" for trichome and root close-ups.

Generation & Consistency Notes

Generation Order Generate Image 09 (hero vGrow shot) first. Generate the apartment room shot (#29) second. Use both as Midjourney --sref references for all subsequent shots. This creates visual consistency across the 62 images.
The vGrow Is Always the Same Unit Every time the vGrow appears: same height, same door, same window, same matte black. If it starts looking different across images, regenerate until it matches #09. Inconsistency breaks the "main character" feeling.
Midjourney Consistency Trick Use --cref [URL of image 09] for character reference on all vGrow shots. Use --sref [URL of image 29] for the apartment room on all lifestyle shots. This is the fastest path to visual consistency.
Trichome Shots Images #47 and #49 require a USB digital microscope or phone macro clip lens — not AI generated. These are real shots taken at harvest. AI trichome images often look wrong. If using AI: add "microscope photography, scientific macro, trichome stalks visible" to the prompt.
Ken Burns Motion All images should be slightly larger than 1920×1080 — generate at 2560×1440 minimum so the editor has room to pan/zoom without losing quality. Midjourney --ar 16:9 at high quality exports enough resolution.
Plant Progression Consistency Images #32, #33, #35, #39, #41 should all show the same plant, same camera angle, same background — just different growth stages. This creates a visible time-lapse effect in the slideshow. Generate them in sequence using --sref of the previous shot.
What NOT to Generate No smiling people, no lifestyle hashtag aesthetics, no bright lit dispensary vibes, no product-on-white backgrounds, no stock photo compositions, no text or overlays in the image. Every frame should look like a still from a festival documentary, not Instagram.
