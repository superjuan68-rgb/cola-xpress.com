The Core Opportunity

VIVOSUN sells tens of thousands of vGrow units. Almost no quality YouTube content exists for it. Someone buying a vGrow right now searches YouTube and finds short, low-production unboxings and a handful of grow logs with no craft angle. Cola-Xpress can own the entire vGrow search space — "vgrow review," "vgrow dwc," "vivosun vgrow setup" — with three well-produced episodes before any competitor notices. That is the fastest route to 1,000 subscribers and the first meaningful backlink signal from YouTube itself.

The cinematic format is not just aesthetic positioning — it is a compression moat. Matching the production level requires real effort. Most cannabis channels will not bother. The moat builds itself.

YouTube has massive gaps in cannabis content — most channels are broad, inconsistent, or low-production. Targeted, specific titles with good watch-time win over high subscriber counts. Step 7 — Channel Trailer
Shown to Non-Subscribers on Channel Home Page

The channel trailer is a 60–90 second video shown to people who find the channel before subscribing. It is not Episode 01 — it is the answer to "why should I subscribe?" Use it to establish the aesthetic and promise in under 90 seconds.

ElementDirection
Length 60–75 seconds. Not 90. Not 2 minutes. The first 15 seconds determine whether a non-subscriber stays. Structure for the decision at 15 seconds, not 60.
Opening (0:00–0:12) The same 12-second sound design open from Episode 01 — silence, then ambient enclosure hum. One frame: the vGrow door closed. That's it. No title card, no music yet. The restraint is the hook.
Statement (0:12–0:25) VO over montage: "Growing became complicated. Not all at once. Gradually — one device, one app, one forum thread at a time. This channel is about what happens when you simplify it. Radically."
Visual sequence (0:25–0:55) Fast montage of the most visually distinct shots from Episode 01: root mass macro, VPD readout at 1.1, day counter progression (Day 1 → Day 28), door-open shot. Ólafur Arnalds register underneath. Each shot 2–3 seconds. No narration, just cuts.
Close (0:55–1:10) VO: "Compact grows. Craft results. Documented." — then 4 seconds of silence on the Cola-Xpress wordmark. No subscribe animation, no "smash that bell." The subscribe button is always visible on YouTube — you don't need to ask.
Thumbnail Use the door-open shot. Dark frame, teal glow from inside the enclosure. "Cola-Xpress" wordmark bottom-left in Cormorant Garamond. No text overlay on the shot itself.

The Search Term Takeover in Practice

Three videos own the vGrow search space: Episode 01 + the "worth it?" Short + one setup explainer. That is 3 pieces of content against near-zero competition. VIVOSUN's unit sales create the search demand. Cola-Xpress provides the only quality answer. The algorithm does the rest.

The site and the channel compound each other. YouTube descriptions link to cola-xpress.com — those are indexed links Google sees. The VPD explainer video drives viewers to the chart page. The chart page ranks in Google and drives new viewers to the channel. The loop closes around the 4-month mark if both tracks execute in parallel.

The cinematic format is the moat. The 9 search terms marked "Own now" will be claimed by the first channel to post quality content targeting them. With the production level already planned and scripted, Cola-Xpress posts that content first. No competitor can replicate the format in the time it takes to film three episodes.
Step 9 — YouTube Studio Settings Checklist
Before You Publish Episode 01
SettingWhereAction
Channel keywords Studio → Settings → Channel → Basic info Paste the keyword string from Step 4 above
Default upload settings Studio → Settings → Upload defaults Set default license to "Standard YouTube License." Set default category to "Howto & Style." Pre-fill tags with the core 8–10 channel tags so you don't forget them per video.
Age restriction Per-video → Audience Set "Made for Kids: No" on every video. Cannabis content requires this. If YouTube auto-restricts, appeal with the Audience setting first.
Monetization Studio → Earn Enable monetization as soon as eligible (1,000 subscribers + 4,000 watch hours). Cannabis content is monetizable — YouTube does not ban cannabis education content. Explicit drug use or sales are different.
End screens Per-video → Editor → End screen Add a "Subscribe" element and a "Video" element (pointing to the most-relevant next video) to every upload. Set at 20 seconds before end. Required on every video before publishing.
Cards Per-video → Editor → Cards Add one info card per video linking to cola-xpress.com. Use the "Link" card type. Place it at the moment in the video where a site resource is referenced in the VO.
Chapters Video description Paste the timestamp chapters from the upload package (ep01-upload-package.html). Chapters improve watch time by letting viewers navigate — YouTube's algorithm reads chapter engagement.
Pinned comment Comments → Pin Within 30 minutes of publishing: post a comment with the full resource list (VPD chart, DWC guide, episode companion page) and pin it. The pinned comment is indexed by YouTube and seen by every viewer who scrolls to comments.
