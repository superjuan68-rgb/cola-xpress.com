Open Loop Architecture — All Three Loops Must Resolve

Loop 01 — Complexity vs. Simplicity — Opens Act I, resolves Act V. "Growing became complicated." → "The complicated era is not over. It's optional."
Loop 02 — The Craft Question — Opens Act II, resolves Act V. "Does automation remove the art?" → "Yes. And no." → "The craft didn't disappear. It got quieter."
Loop 03 — The Day Counter — Opens Act I (DAY 01), sustains through Act IV (Day 3/7/14/28), resolves Act V (DAY 08 →). Builds series architecture into the story.
Cold Open / Act I
The Weight
0:00 – 3:30
Scene 01 — Sound Design Open / No Visuals Yet
SOUND
Black screen. No visual.
Layer in slowly over 8 seconds:
— Low fan hum. Not a grow tent fan — something architectural, infrastructural.

Post-Production Notes for Editor

Color Grade Target Dark, cool-neutral base. Skin tones: irrelevant (no on-cam presenter). Shadows: lifted — not crushed. The enclosure teal (#38b4c8) should be the only saturated color in the frame; everything else is desaturated. Reference: Succession S4 interiors × Apple product films. The visual hierarchy is: teal glow → warm text → everything else neutral.
Music Direction Three phases. Act I (complexity montage): no music — ambient tension, the clutter's own sound. Act II–III (analogy + reveal): a single sustained low tone, barely music — more like the sound of a system running correctly. Acts IV–V (evidence + manifesto): the tone develops very slowly, minimal melody, resolves to near-silence by Act V close. Reference: Ólafur Arnalds, Max Richter low-register works, Johann Johannsson. Not film score — architecture music. Budget option: Epidemic Sound "Dark Minimal" or "Nordic" category, 90+ BPM avoided entirely.
Typography System Cormorant Garamond 700 for title cards and the DAY XX counter — white, centered, sized to fill roughly 20% of frame height. Manrope 700 for data overlays (VPD readings, app notifications) — smaller, positioned lower-third. Letter-spaced caps for labels. All text: white on black or white on very dark field. No outlines, no drop shadows. If legibility requires a shadow, the background shot needs to be darker.
Pacing Rules The silences are not errors. Do not fill them. Every beat marked as "2 seconds" should be 2 seconds minimum — err long. The film's pacing is the argument: the quiet is what the enclosure enables, and the edit should embody it. Fast-cut rhythm is explicitly wrong for this project. If the cut feels too slow to a YouTube-trained eye, it is probably exactly right.
Voiceover Performance Notes Quiet. Measured. Not hushed — confident and quiet. No vocal fry. No performative pauses. No smile in the voice. The register is someone who has thought about this for sixty days and is telling you what they found, not pitching you something. Reference cadence: Werner Herzog, not Casey Neistat. Record in a dead room. No reverb in post.
Chapter Markers — Align to Upload Package 0:00 The Weight / 3:30 The Idea / 6:30 The Reveal / 10:30 The Evidence / 14:30 The Manifesto. Chapter titles in the YouTube description should match these exactly. See ep01-upload-package.html for full description copy.
B-Roll Priority List (Shoot Order) 1. The door-open shot (Act III, Scene 08) — most important visual in the film, allocate maximum time.
2. Root mass macro (Act II, Scene 06 + Act IV, Scene 11) — white, healthy, dense — requires good light and macro lens.
3. The living room wide (Act III, Scene 10) — room must be set-dressed cleanly, no extraneous items in frame.
4. Complexity montage items (Act I) — pH pen, nutrient bottles, phone at 2:47 AM — all close, all controlled light.
5. Day counter plant progression (Act IV) — capture each milestone same angle, same light, same composition.
