Cannabis Drying + Curing Environment Targets
Post-harvest environment for quality preservation
Terpene retention  ·  Mold prevention  ·  Smooth cure
Drying
Phase 01  ·  7–14 days
Temperature
60–70°F
15–21°C  ·  cool and stable
Humidity
55–65%
RH  ·  slow even dry
Light
Dark
No UV  ·  protects terpenes
Drying requirements
Airflow: gentle indirect circulation — no fans blowing directly on buds
Hang whole branches upside-down or use drying rack for trimmed buds
Avoid below 55% RH — drying too fast locks in chlorophyll and harshness
Avoid above 70% RH — mold risk on dense buds within 24–48 hours
Ready to jar when
Small stems snap cleanly rather than bend. Outer buds feel dry to touch but interior still slightly soft. Hay smell beginning to fade — never jar if still strong hay smell.

Curing
Phase 02  ·  2–8+ weeks
Temperature
60–70°F
15–21°C  ·  same as drying
Humidity
58–65%
RH  ·  ideal: 62%
Container
Glass
Mason jars  ·  62% Boveda
Burping schedule
Week 1: open jars 2× daily for 10–15 min — release CO2 and moisture
Week 2: once daily — smell improves, hay fades, terpenes emerge
Weeks 3–4: every 2–3 days — most cures are smoke-ready here
Weeks 6–8: full terpene development, maximum smoothness, peak cure
Cure is complete when
Hay smell fully gone. Strain-specific aroma present. Smoke is smooth with no harshness on exhale. Hygrometer inside jar reads 58–65% RH stable between burps.
