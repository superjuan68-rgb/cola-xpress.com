Facebook Cannabis Content Policy — Read First

Organic posts are permitted. Facebook allows cannabis cultivation education, grow documentation, legal information, and reference content as organic page posts. What is restricted: paid advertising for cannabis products or services (including cultivation equipment sold via affiliate), direct purchase CTAs, and any content that facilitates illegal activity in the viewer's jurisdiction.

What this means for Cola-Xpress: All planned content — grow documentation, VPD charts, DWC guides, NY home grow law, cinematic episodes — is fully permissible as organic content. Do not run paid ads. Do not use "buy," "shop," or affiliate links in post copy. The page is categorised as "Website" not "Cannabis Dispensary" — this avoids triggering cannabis-specific content restrictions on the category level. Set the page age gate to 18+.

Step 5 — Content Pillars
Four Content Types. Post on Rotation.

All four pillars reinforce each other. Pillar 1 builds the audience. Pillar 2 drives traffic to the site. Pillar 3 earns shares (organic reach). Pillar 4 signals authority and draws in the NY-specific audience.

Pillar 01
Grow Journal Updates
Real photo + real data from the active grow. Day number, one environmental reading, one observation. No fluff. These are the posts that get saved and shared — growers compare their data against yours.
"Day 14. VPD 1.1 kPa. Root mass visible through the reservoir wall for the first time. The plant has never been stressed."
Pillar 02
Site Guide Links
Shares of the chart and guide pages at cola-xpress.com. Facebook link previews auto-generate a card with the page title, image, and URL. One sentence of context, then the link. These drive direct site traffic and earn the indexed links Google sees.
"The VPD targets shift every two weeks. We charted the full range by stage — kPa, formula, and a temperature/RH grid in one place."
Pillar 03
Short-Form Data Posts
Text-only or text + single photo. One specific number, one specific observation, one specific fact. No tutorial structure. These perform well on Facebook because they invite comments ("what's your target?") and get saved for reference.
"The stem snap test for harvest readiness: bend a small branch. Clean snap, dry sound = ready. Flex without snapping = needs more time."
Pillar 04
NY Home Grow Resources
NY-specific content: law updates, apartment constraints, what changed in MRTA, what home growers in NYC actually face. Highly shareable within the NY cannabis community and not produced by any other Facebook page with craft positioning.
"New York home grow law in one paragraph: 3 plants per adult, 6 per household, any space you occupy. No permit required. Here's what the law actually says."
Step 6 — First 8 Posts
Copy-ready. Post in this exact order.

Post Timing Logic

Post 1 is the brand introduction — it anchors everything that follows. Posts 2–4 establish the three content pillars in the first week. Posts 5–8 build rhythm and demonstrate that this page posts regularly. Facebook's algorithm rewards pages that post 3–5 times per week with consistent engagement — the first two weeks set the pattern. Best times for cannabis/gardening content on Facebook: Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9pm local time.

Facebook Groups vs. Page — the difference

The page builds the brand identity. The groups build the audience. Most Facebook page growth for niche content happens through groups — someone sees a useful comment, clicks through to the page, and follows. The page has to be ready to receive that traffic with consistent content before you start group participation. That's why the 8 posts above run first.
