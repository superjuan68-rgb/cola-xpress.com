Color Grade Direction

Dark, cool-neutral base. The teal LED from the vGrow cabinet is the practical key color — let it influence the grade, don't correct it out. Skin tones warm but not orange. Shadows lifted slightly — not crushed black. Deep blacks in cabinet shots are intentional. Reference: high-end spirits or coffee documentary grade. NOT bright/saturated YouTube creator aesthetic.

Music Direction

Minimal electronic or ambient. Slow, deliberate, infrastructure-feeling. NOT: trap, hip-hop, stoner music, upbeat anything. Suggested search on Epidemic Sound: "dark minimal", "subtle tension", "cinematic ambient". Music should support the silence — not fill it.

Text Overlay / Lower Thirds

  • Font: Manrope (display), Cormorant Garamond (quotes/headlines)
  • Color: White (#ffffff) on dark backgrounds, accent red (#9f3028) for emphasis
  • Data labels: white, letter-spaced caps, small (Manrope 700)
  • HUD overlay: composite from hud-overlay.png, position bottom-left, Screen mode ~90% opacity
  • Chapter title cards: black full-frame, Cormorant Garamond white, cut to black hold for 0.5s

Pacing Notes

Do This

  • Hold long silences on the cabinet
  • Let the bubble/hum ambient audio breathe
  • Slow cuts in B-roll sequences
  • Match cut on hands (cold open → Act 3)
  • Pause after key philosophical lines
  • Build to the tension point in Act 3 slowly

Do Not Do This

  • Fast cuts, jump cuts in VO segments
  • Add music over the silence beats
  • Cut away from the cabinet before the VO finishes
  • Use any brightness/saturation LUT preset
  • Add intro bumpers, outros with channel music
  • Speed ramp any footage

Deliverables

  • Proxy/web cut: CX_EP01_WEB.mp4 (same edit, compressed for web review)
  • .SRT captions file (auto-generated + corrected for cannabis terminology)
  • Project file (.prproj or .drp) delivered in case of revisions
  • Thumbnail: thumbnail-ep01.png (delivered by brand team — just confirm it's correct)

Role 03

Scriptwriter — Episodes 02–07 (Future)

Series Arc Overview

ColaXpress Grow Series documents a full cannabis grow from seed to cured jar using the VIVOSUN vGrow DWC setup. Episode 01 (scripted by brand team) establishes the tone, question, and characters. Episodes 02–07 follow the same format — each anchored to a grow stage with a specific craft-relevant question.

Episode Arc (Reference)

EP 01 — Day 01 · Seedling · "Can automation produce craft cannabis?" ✓ (completed)
EP 02 — Day 08 · First roots into reservoir · "Does the plant know it's in a machine?"
EP 03 — Day 21 · Early veg · LST training begins · "Where does craft happen in a cabinet?"
EP 04 — Day 35 · Pre-flower transition · "What VPD looks like when the plant changes"
EP 05 — Day 50 · Full flower · "The decision the automation can't make: when to flush"
EP 06 — Harvest day · Trichome evaluation · "The harvest is not the finish line"
EP 07 — 6 weeks post-harvest · Opening the jar · "Did the automation produce craft?"

Voice and Tone Requirements

Write Like This

  • Precise language — name the metric, give the number
  • Short declarative sentences in on-cam segments
  • Longer, slower sentences in VO (they breathe on screen)
  • Questions that have real tension — no rhetorical filler
  • Respect the audience's intelligence — no over-explaining
  • Craft = attention, not a product descriptor

Never Write This

  • "Hey guys" / "What's up everyone"
  • "Smash that subscribe button"
  • Stoner slang, weed jokes, pun-based headers
  • Hype language ("INSANE results", "you won't believe")
  • Filler content that isn't about craft or the grow
  • Any content that treats cannabis as recreational only

Script Format

Match the format in ep01-script.html exactly. Each scene tagged: ON CAM / VO / B-ROLL / DIRECTION. Act structure with timecode targets. Editor notes at the end. Delivered as HTML using the provided template — brand team will handle all visual production of the document.

Research Required Per Episode

  • Actual grow data for that stage (provided by brand team from the real grow journal)
  • VPD target for the specific growth stage
  • One primary craft-relevant concept to explain (e.g., flush timing, trichome maturity, cure science)
  • One tension point (something that could go wrong at this stage)
  • Bridge to next episode — tease the next question
