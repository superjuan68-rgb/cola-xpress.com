Trichome Development Stages — When to Harvest
Visual guide to trichome color under a loupe or microscope
Use a 60–100x jeweler's loupe or USB microscope to inspect calyxes
Stage 01
Clear
Early–mid flower  ·  Wk 8–11
Trichomes are fully transparent. Capitate-stalked heads just forming. THC and other cannabinoids are still synthesizing — essentially zero potency. Plant is still putting on mass.
ImmatureNo potency
Too early — do not harvest
Stage 02
Cloudy / Milky
Late flower  ·  Wk 11–14
Trichome heads turn opaque white-milky. THC is at its highest concentration. Harvest now for a more cerebral, energetic, uplifting effect. Most sativa-leaning growers aim here.
Peak THCUpliftingCerebral
Prime window — harvest for energetic effect
Stage 03
Cloudy + Amber
Ripening  ·  Wk 13–16
Mix of milky and amber heads — typically 70-80% cloudy, 20-30% amber. THC beginning to convert to CBN. Balanced effect: cerebral high with body relaxation. Most popular harvest window for indica-dominant grows.
BalancedRelaxingFull spectrum
Peak full-spectrum — most popular window
Stage 04
Mostly Amber
Over-ripe  ·  Wk 15+
50%+ amber trichomes. THC has heavily degraded to CBN. Sedative, couch-lock effect. Smell may become hay-like. Bud is past peak potency but prized for sleep or pain applications.
SedativeHigh CBNPast peak
Harvest now — potency declining
